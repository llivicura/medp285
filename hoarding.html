Hoarding
Black Friday shopping for toilet paper during a global pandemic
Due to mass buying of food, the elderly and essential workers struggle to find any remaining products
❮ ❯

As news of a new virus with the potential to affect nations worldiwde spread, fear caused hoards of people to travel to their nearest stores and buy any and all necessities in bulks. Within days, disinfectants and hand sanitizers were sold out and there was a dire need of masks. It was obvious at this point people feared for their lives and were willing to selfishly buy what they deemed important without regarding the needs of others. In addition to cleaning products and masks, a certain product would also be wiped off store shelves and even trend among social media

For reasons unknown, many stores had begun to report that people were purchasing large amounts of toilet paper. Although it sounded ridiculous at first, videos and images begun to surface in social media showing these people taking whatever they could, almost as if they were partaking in a Black Friday sale. As children we're taught that adults are wise and mature, but that certainly doesn't seem to be the case when there's videos of them fighting over toilet paper. To make matters worse, they're emptying their local grocery stores making it more difficult for essential workers to shop for food.
